Thoughtstream Card — B+D Mashup

Three ways to blend B's context-first anchoring with D's image-as-hero treatment. Pick the variant you prefer and generate an implementation prompt.

BD-1 — Full Context Banner + Dark Date Strip When a post has context (adventure/project): full-width dark banner at the top exactly as in option B — you can't miss the adventure link. Images below it lead with a dark date+location strip underneath (from D). Text-only posts get a simple compact date header. Body always appears after the image area.

✓ Adventure link is unmissable — full-width, high-contrast.
✓ Date is clearly legible — dark strip, not buried in footer.
✓ Text-only posts stay lightweight without a heavy empty banner.
✗ Two dark/heavy elements (banner + date strip) in succession may feel dense.

BD-2 — Thick Left Border + Context Chip Context shown as a thick coloured left border (green = adventure, navy = project) plus a clickable chip that appears just below the image's date strip. Less aggressive than a full banner — the border is a subtle persistent signal. Images still lead full-width (D). The chip gives a clear clickable element for context navigation without taking up a whole row.

✓ Colour-coded border lets you scan the feed at a glance by context type.
✓ Context chip is clearly clickable without the visual weight of a full banner.
✓ Consistent layout — border appears even for text-only posts with context.
✗ Context chip is below the fold if the image is tall — might miss it.

BD-3 — Thin Accent Bar + Magazine The most restrained version. When context is present, a thin coloured left strip + pale tinted row shows the adventure/project name as a small inline link — not a full banner, not a chip bubble. Magazine-style images with a semi-transparent date strip overlay. Text-only posts just get a simple date header.

✓ Context is present and clickable but not visually loud.
✓ Semi-transparent date strip keeps the image feeling uninterrupted.
✓ Most consistent layout between text and image posts.
✗ Context link is subtle — might be overlooked by visitors.
✗ Less brutalist than the full-border BD-1 — softer feel overall.
